Module: $mod$ | Topic: $topic$ | Field Extraction

Data structure within Splunk is generally something which is computed at the time a search needs it. This is often done through the use of regular expressions. The Interactive Field Extractor (IFX) allows you to extract fields using regular expression without having to know the regular expression language itself.

To this point we've only discussed a few fields, specifically those that are "index-time" fields, or fields that are associated with the actual events at the time of indexing such as _raw, _time, host, index, linecount, source, sourcetype, and splunk_server. You'll need other fields to be available for you though in order to create the most useful and efficient searches.

There are some types of data which Splunk understands natively, and many others that have already been defined and made available through Splunk apps and "Technology Add-ons" on Splunkbase. However, it's useful to understand the process of field extraction and how to create your own. Every Splunk user is capable of defining their own field extractions (or "schema") to be used for searches.

The following exercise is OPTIONAL. Below you'll see a walkthrough of the steps needed to extract fields as well as the Interactive Field Extractor at the bottom of the page so that you can walk through the instructions without leaving the tutorial.

  • 1. Select the Sourcetype
  • 2. Choose the Extraction Method
  • 3. Select and Name Fields
  • 4. Validate Fields
  • 5. Save Fields

  • At the Search Bar, run the following search, to bring back the Access Combined data (using "All Time" for the Time Range):
  • index=$idx$ sourcetype=$stype$
  • Next, scroll down to the bottom of the page. On the left hand side, click on +Extract New Fields
  • Select an appropriate sample event from the search results generated for you


  • Click the "Next" button

  • Click the "Regular Expression" method
  • Click "Next"

  • Select the "GET" OR "POST" term found within your selected event (only one of the terms will be present) and provide http_method as the Field Name


  • Click Add Extraction
  • Review the highlighted terms (examples: GET and POST), and verify that Splunk is extracting that information from the proper location in the events


  • Click "Next"

  • You can hone your selection by reviewing the highlighted items and clicking the "X" to remove any strays that Splunk interpreted incorrectly


  • Click "Next"

  • Review your work and click "Finish"
